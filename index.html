About Me

Professionally

      I currently work as a Teaching Assistant (TA) at UC Berkeley Extension Full-Time Coding Program in San Francisco, CA (did the giant background with the Golden Gate Bridge give it away?). As a TA, I do a lot. Most importantly, I work to help students learn not just how to write code, but how to think as programmers. I also write coding challenges designed to help students retain and practice concepts and tools covered in class (viewable in my portfolio).

     Working as a TA, I learn something new everyday. I am constantly picking up new insights from colleagues and students on how to think about and how to approach code. It’s a lot of fun. Even more, I’m able to practice more code in addition to the projects I work on in my spare time.

     Teaching code has taught me to think about code in ways I would not have without the experience. I’m constantly forced to step out of my comfort zone, made to solve problems from others’ point of view rather than approaching them in the ways I normally would.

     As a developer, I’m always ready--even excited--to take on new challenges. I’m a full-stack developer, and have built full-stack apps, but I have a small preference for the front end (I’m a sucker for seeing the results of my work come to life on the screen). My two favorite technologies/libraries are React and Sass/CSS. That being said, I always love a new challenge. I bet I can figure out whatever you throw at me 😁.

Personally

I was born and raised in San Francisco, CA. I’ve loved the Bay Area my whole life, and it’ll always be home.

I went to college in Worcester (pronounced Woosta), MA, where I got my Master’s in Public Administration. After spending a few months working the California State Senate, I realized I was working in the wrong industry. I started to teach myself to code, and the rest is history.

I love to laugh and I love to have fun. I take my work seriously, but I always do my best to have fun while I’m working. It keeps me motivated and sharp. I’m pretty easy to get along with.

I can have fun doing almost anything, but some of my top loves include video games (Fortnite is my current favorite), basketball (go Dubs), Harry Potter and walking my two bloodhounds.
