About Me

I thought I was going to go into politics almost my entire life. Not to run for office or anything, but just to work in some kind of political environment. For years in high school and in college, I hardly questioned that idea.

I followed the political track, and I followed it closely. I studied sociology as an undergrad in college, and spent a Summer as an intern in the office of Congresswoman Jackie Speier (CA-14). Later I got my Master’s in Public Administration at Clark University in Worcester, MA (it’s pronounced Woosta, and it was really cold!).

The summer after I graduated, I quickly landed a job in the California State Senate with the Democratic Majority Leader, Senator Bill Monning, where I worked as a Field Representative.

It was a few weeks into this job that I began to realize I might be in the wrong industry. I found myself trying extra hard to motivate myself at work. I thought, maybe I just needed to give the job some more time, to see if I liked it. But sometimes you just know. I gave the job a total of three months before I knew that it was not something I wanted to do for the rest of my life.

However, during that time, I found something I did want to do for the rest of my life. During the time I began to have doubts as to whether I wanted to continue to work in the State Senate, and more broadly, in politics, I began to write code.
